    \({\large \textbf {4.2 Worksheet Exercises}}\)
    🔗
    1. Express the solution to \(A \vec {x} = \vec {b}\) in parametric vector form.
    🔗
    🔗
    (a) \(A = \) \(\begin {pmatrix} 1 \amp 3 \amp 4 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 1 \end {pmatrix}\) \(\vec {b} = \) \(\begin {pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end {pmatrix}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    (b) \(A = \) \(\begin {pmatrix} 1 \amp 3 \amp 4 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 1 \end {pmatrix}\) \(\vec {b} = \) \(\begin {pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end {pmatrix}\)
    🔗
    2. Example construction exercises.
    🔗
    🔗
    (a) Give an example of a non zero 2 \(\times\) 3 matrix \(A\) such that \(\vec {x} = /begin {pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 1 /end {pmatrix} \) is a solution of \(A \vec {x} = \vec {0}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    (b) Give an example of a non trivial solution to \(A \vec {x} = \vec {0}\text{,}\) where \(A = \begin {pmatrix} 2 \amp 5 \\ 0 \amp 0 \\ 4 \amp 10 \end {pmatrix}\)
    🔗
    3. Indicate whether the statements are true or false.
    🔗
    (a) If \(\vec {x}\) is a non-trivial solution of \(A \vec {x} = \vec {0}\text{,}\) then all of the entries of \(\vec {x}\) are non-zero.
    🔗
    🔗
    (b) if \(A \vec {x} = \vec {b}\) and \(A \vec {y} = \vec {b}\text{,}\) then \(A(\vec {x} - \vec {y}) = \vec {0}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    (c) Any \(3 \times 2 \) \(A\) with two pivotal positions has a non trivial solution to \(A \vec {x} = 0\)
