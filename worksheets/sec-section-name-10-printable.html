    \({\large \textbf {Poll Question}}\)
    🔗
    Consider the following matrix equation:
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} (A + B)^2 = A^2 + B^2 + 2AB \end{equation*}
    Assume \(A, B, C\) are all \(n \times n\text{.}\) When does this equation hold? Always, sometimes, never?
    🔗
    \({\Large \textbf {6.1 Matrix Addition and Multiplication}} \)
    🔗
    \({\large \textbf {Addition and Scalar Multiplication}} \)
    🔗
    Suppose \(A\) and \(B\) are \(m \times n\) matrices.
    🔗
    • the entry of \(A\) in row \(i\) and column \(j\) is \(a_{i,j}\)
      🔗
    • the entry of \(B\) in row \(i\) and column \(j\) is \(b_{i,j}\)
      🔗
    Then:
    🔗
    • The entries of \(A + B\) are \(a_{i,j} + b_{i,j}\text{.}\)
      🔗
    • If \(c \in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) then the entries of \(cA\) are \(c a_{i,j}\text{.}\)
      🔗
    \({\large \textbf {Example}} \)
    🔗
    Suppose
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} 1 0 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 2 \amp 6 \end{pmatrix} + c \begin{pmatrix} 2 \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp k \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 9 \amp 4 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 2 \amp 14 \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
    What are the values of \(c\) and \(k\text{?}\)
    🔗
    \({\large \textbf{Multiplication}}\)
    🔗
    \(A, B, C\) be matrices of dimensions needed for matrix multiplication to be defined, and \(A\) is \(m \times n\text{.}\)
    🔗
    1. \(\displaystyle (AB)C = A(BC)\)
      🔗
    2. \(\displaystyle A(B + C) = AB + AC\)
      🔗
    3. \(\displaystyle (A + B)C = AC + BC\)
      🔗
    4. \(\displaystyle I_m A = A I_n\)
      🔗
    \({\large \textbf {Warnings:}}\)
    🔗
    1. \(AB \neq BA \) in general
      🔗
      🔗
    2. \(AB = AC\) does not mean \(B = C\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. \(AB = 0\) does not mean that either \(A = 0\) or \(B = 0\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    \({\large \textbf {Example}}\)
    🔗
    If
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 0 \end{pmatrix} , B = \begin{pmatrix} k \amp 0 \\ 1 \amp 0 \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
    \(\textup{then what values}\) of \(k\) (if any) satisfy \(AB = 0\text{?}\)
